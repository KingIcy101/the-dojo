9. Sales & Outreach Methodology
Outreach Philosophy
Halo's outreach approach emphasizes indirect positioning and value-first engagement. The goal is to create
curiosity and demonstrate capability before asking for anything.
Cold Calling Framework
Daily Target: 50+ calls per day
Approach: Indirect positioning—initially as potential patient, then transition to business discussion
The 'Confused Patient' Approach:
1. Call the practice as if you're a potential patient
2. Note response time, professionalism, and booking process
3. If they miss the call or mishandle it, use this as proof of the problem
4. Follow up with business offer, referencing the gap you just demonstrated
Cold Email Strategy
Platform: Saleshandy
Domain: trygohalomarketing.com (protects main domain)
Volume: Multi-touch sequences with warmup periods
Email Sequence Example: 'Confused Patient' to 'Marketing Audit'
Email 1 (The Hook):
Subject: Question about new patient appointments
'Hi [Name], I tried calling about a new patient appointment but got voicemail. Do you have availability this
week or are you pretty booked up? Thanks, Matt'
Email 2 (The Reveal - Send 3 Days Later if No Response):
Subject: Re: Question about new patient appointments
'[Name], Full transparency—I wasn't calling as a patient. I'm Matt from Halo Marketing, and we specialize
in filling schedules for dental practices. I sent that first email because most practices we work with are
losing 30-40% of potential patients to the exact scenario I just created—missed calls, full voicemails, slow
response times. I put together a quick audit of your current patient acquisition funnel. No charge, no
strings. Just wanted to show you what we're seeing. Would Thursday at 2pm work for a 15-minute call?
Matt'
Psychology: This sequence demonstrates the problem (missed opportunity), establishes authority (you
spotted it), and offers value (free audit) before asking for anything.

11. Psychological Selling Principles
These frameworks should be applied across all sales and marketing communications:
1. Reciprocity
Give value before asking for anything. Free audits, free first month, free insights—all create psychological
obligation.
Application: Suzanne's 'first month free' deal created commitment before payment.
2. Loss Aversion
People fear losing more than they desire gaining. Frame decisions in terms of what they'll lose by not acting.
Application: 'Most practices lose 30-40% of potential patients to missed calls.'
3. The Contrast Principle
Never present pricing in isolation. Always contrast with higher alternatives.
Application: 'Most agencies charge $5-8K monthly for less... our systems are built for $1,950.'
4. Anchoring
The first number presented becomes the reference point for all subsequent judgments.
Application: Lead with the $2,950 telehealth pricing to make $1,950 standard pricing feel like a deal.
5. Social Proof
People follow the actions of others, especially similar others.
Application: 'We're currently working with practices just like yours in the Maryland area...'
6. Scarcity + Urgency
Limited availability increases perceived value. But it must be REAL scarcity, not manufactured.
Application: 'We're onboarding 8 practices per quarter to maintain quality. We have 2 slots left for Q2.'
7. Authority
People defer to experts. Establish credibility through specialization and local knowledge.
Application: Matt's local Maryland background and healthcare-only focus establish authority.
