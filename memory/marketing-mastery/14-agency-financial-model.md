## The Unit Economics of One Client

At $1,950/month retainer, what does one client actually cost and return?

### Cost to Acquire (CAC)

| Channel | Estimated CAC |
|---|---|
| Cold calling (SDR time) | $150–$300 |
| Cold email (tool cost + time) | $80–$200 |
| Referral | $50–$150 (referral credit) |
| Agency's own FB ads | $200–$500 |
| Blended average | ~$200–$350 |

At $350 CAC, payback period = 6 days of retainer revenue. Excellent.

### Cost to Deliver (CTS — Cost to Serve)

Per client per month at current scale:

| Expense | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Media buyer time (1–2 hours/week) | $200–$400 |
| Account manager time | $150–$300 |
| GHL sub-account | $~30 |
| Bland.ai (AI calls per client) | $20–$80 |
| Reporting + tools allocation | $30–$50 |
| **Total CTS per client** | **$430–$860** |

**Gross margin per client:** $1,950 − $650 avg CTS = **$1,300/month (~67% margin)**

At scale (20+ clients per AM, better tooling), margin improves to 70–75%.

### Lifetime Value (LTV)

Average agency client retention:
- Industry average: 6–9 months
- Top agencies: 18–24+ months
- Halo's target: 12+ months

At 12-month average retention:
- LTV = $1,950 × 12 = **$23,400 per client**
- Minus CAC ($300) and CTS ($7,800) = **$15,300 net profit per client**

**LTV:CAC ratio = ~67:1** — extremely healthy (anything above 3:1 is good)

## What Drives the Math: Churn vs. Growth

The most important financial lever is **churn rate** — not client acquisition.

### Scenario A: 10% monthly churn, 10 new clients/month
- Month 1: 10 clients
- Month 6: ~55 clients (but losing 5.5/month by now)
- Month 12: ~65 clients (growth stalls because churn erases additions)

### Scenario B: 5% monthly churn, 10 new clients/month
- Month 1: 10 clients
- Month 6: ~70 clients
- Month 12: ~110 clients

**Same acquisition rate. Half the churn. 70% more clients in month 12.**

This is why retention and results delivery is the #1 priority. Not more cold calls.

**Churn reduction tactics:**
1. Results — the most basic but most important
2. Proactive communication — clients don't churn agencies they hear from regularly
3. Lock in annual contracts (removes month-to-month decision)
4. Create switching costs — once GHL is fully set up for their practice, it's painful to move
5. Quarterly goals — always have a "next chapter" for every client relationship

## Cash Flow Considerations

### The Cash Flow Advantage of Retainers

Unlike project-based agencies, Halo collects payment BEFORE service begins (Matt's rule). This is a massive cash flow advantage:

- Day 1: Client signs and pays $1,950
- Day 5: Ads go live (cost starts flowing)
- Day 30: Client pays again
- Net: Halo is always cash positive, never fronting costs

Compare to project agencies that invoice after completion and wait 30–60 days for payment. Retainers are infinitely better.

### Build a Cash Reserve Early

At 10 clients, the temptation is to spend all margin on growth. Resist.

**Target: 2–3 months of operating expenses in reserve before making each major hire.**

Why:
- Client churn happens in batches (2 clients leave same week)
- Tools/platforms sometimes have unexpected costs
- A team member leaves and needs to be replaced quickly
- Reserve buys you calm, strategic decision-making vs. panic hiring

## The Exit Scenario (For Context)

This section isn't relevant now, but worth understanding for motivation:

Marketing agencies typically sell for **3–6x annual recurring revenue (ARR)** or **8–12x EBITDA** (earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, amortization).

At 150 clients with $293K MRR:
- ARR = $3.5M
- EBITDA (assuming 50% margin) = $1.75M/year
- At 10x EBITDA = **$17.5M exit value**

Even at a more modest 100 clients ($195K MRR):
- ARR = $2.34M
- EBITDA = ~$1.2M
- At 10x EBITDA = **$12M exit value**

In 3–5 years. From a $0 starting point. With two 24-year-old founders.

This is why healthcare marketing agencies are such a powerful business model — predictable recurring revenue at high margins from a massively underserved market.

## The One Number to Watch Daily

At Halo's current stage, the one number that predicts everything else:

**Discovery calls held per week.**

Track it. If it's < 5/week, the pipeline isn't moving fast enough. If it's 10+/week, you're going to have a great month 30–45 days from now (the typical lag from discovery call to signed contract to onboarded client).

Everything else (ads, GHL, Bland.ai, systems) is in service of this one number. More discovery calls = more closes = more clients = more revenue = more impact.
