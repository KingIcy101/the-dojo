## The Mindset: Volume + Consistency Beats Everything

Agency owners who've scaled fast (Iman Gadzhi, Jordan Platten, and their successors in the healthcare niche) all say the same thing: **outreach volume solves every early problem.** When you're at 50 calls/day + a multi-touch email sequence, you WILL find clients. The question is just timing.

One call per day isn't a strategy. 50 calls/day with a structured follow-up system is a machine.

## The Prospect List — Who to Call

### Best Targets for Halo

**Tier 1 (Hottest):**
- Practices running Facebook/Google ads but clearly not doing it well (bad landing pages, weak copy, no follow-up)
- Practices with 3.5–4.2 stars on Google (frustrated with reputation but not addressing it)
- Practices that recently opened or expanded (new locations = need for new patients)
- Practices with obvious website problems (old site, no booking button, no mobile optimization)

**Tier 2 (Warm):**
- Established single-provider practices (chiropractors, dentists) with no visible digital marketing
- Telehealth providers with no ad presence but active on social media
- Practices that just left a competitor agency (check their Facebook ad history using Meta Ad Library)

**Tier 3 (Cold):**
- Any licensed chiropractor, dentist, or telehealth provider within target niche

### How to Build the List

**Free methods:**
- Google Maps: Search "chiropractor [city]" → list every result → find their website, phone, email
- Facebook Ad Library (library.facebook.com): Search by advertiser category = "Health" → find practices running ads (they have budget!)
- LinkedIn: Search "chiropractor [state]" or "telehealth provider" → DM or find email via Apollo
- Psychology Today / Zocdoc / Healthgrades directories: Massive lists of providers with contact info

**Paid methods:**
- Apollo.io ($49–$99/month): Search by specialty, geography, company size. Exports email + phone
- ZoomInfo or D&B (expensive, but enterprise lists) — not needed at Halo's current stage
- DataAxle / InfoUSA: Healthcare-specific lists available for purchase

**Volume target:** 100–200 contacts/week added to the list keeps the SDR fed at 50 calls/day.

## Cold Calling — The Script System

### The 3-Part Framework
Every cold call needs:
1. **Permission + reason to talk** (10 seconds)
2. **Problem hook** — show you know their world (20–30 seconds)
3. **Ask for the next step** — discovery call (10 seconds)

**Total call: 60–90 seconds if they engage**

---

### The Core Cold Call Script (Chiropractic)

**[Ring ring — receptionist answers]**

"Hi, is [Doctor's name] available? ... Great, this is [Name] calling — I'm with Halo Marketing, we work exclusively with chiropractic practices. Do you have about 60 seconds? ... 

[IF YES OR HESITANT:]

Great, I'll be quick. We work specifically with chiropractors — we run Facebook and Google campaigns that bring in 15–30 new patients a month, and we use an automated follow-up system so no leads go cold. We're just starting to work with practices in [their area] and I wanted to see if it made sense to have a short conversation.

Are you the right person to talk to about marketing, or would that be you or someone else at the practice?"

---

### Handling Gatekeepers

**Receptionist:** "What's this about?"
→ "I work with chiropractic practices on patient acquisition. Is Dr. [Name] typically the one who handles marketing decisions, or is there someone else?"

**Receptionist:** "He/she doesn't take marketing calls."
→ "I completely understand — most of the doctors we work with said the same thing before we connected. Can I ask — does the practice run any Facebook or Google ads currently?"
→ [If yes:] "Interesting — who manages that for them?"
→ [If no:] "That's actually why I'm calling. We work specifically with practices like yours who aren't doing paid advertising yet. Would it be possible to leave a voicemail for the doctor, or is there an email I could send something to?"

**Get through to doctor — they say "not interested" before you've said much:**
→ "Totally fair — I'll be honest, if your schedule is already full, this call probably doesn't make sense. Is new patient volume something you're actively trying to grow, or are you at capacity right now?"

[Either gets a real answer or a conversation.]

---

### Handling Common Objections

**"We already have a marketing company."**
→ "That's great — how's it working for you? Are you seeing consistent new patients from it?"
→ If "yes, it's great": "Perfect — I won't waste your time then. Can I ask what platform you use? We work with a lot of practices and it's helpful context."
→ If "not really" or hesitation: "That's actually where we come in a lot — we work alongside or take over from agencies that weren't delivering. What's been the main frustration?"

**"We don't have the budget for marketing."**
→ "I hear that. Can I ask — if you added 10 new patients this month and each was worth $2,000 to your practice over 2 years, would $1,950/month be a hard number to justify?"
→ OR: "Would it make sense to at least have a 20-minute call to see if the numbers work? There's no commitment."

**"We get all our patients from referrals."**
→ "Referrals are gold — most of our best clients said the same thing before they started. The practices that run ads usually find they get MORE referrals because their name is everywhere locally. Are you trying to grow, or are you happy with current volume?"

**"Send me something in email."**
→ "Absolutely — what's the best email for the doctor?" [Get it]
→ Then: "Quick question before I let you go — just so I make sure I send the right thing: is new patient volume something you're actively working to grow right now?"

**"I'm not interested."**
→ "No problem at all. One quick question — is that because timing isn't right, or because you've had a bad experience with marketing agencies before?"
→ [Genuine curiosity. Learn from it. Even "bad agency experience" is intel.]

## Cold Email System

### The "Confused Patient" Approach (Halo's current strategy — keep it)

**What it is:** Instead of emailing as "Halo Marketing Agency," you email acting as someone who was referred to their practice or is a confused potential patient, then pivot to the offer. More conversational, less filterable by the "marketing email" mental filter.

**Example:**

Subject: Quick question

"Hi Dr. [Name],

I came across [Practice Name] while looking for [chiropractic care / dental] options in [City]. I noticed you don't seem to be running ads or have an active patient acquisition system — which surprised me given the practice's reputation.

I run a marketing agency called Halo that works exclusively with [chiropractors / dentists], and we typically get practices 15–30 new patients a month through Facebook and Google.

Worth a 20-minute call to see if it's a fit?

— [Name]"

---

### Multi-Touch Email Sequence (5-Email Cadence)

**Email 1 (Day 0):** The confused patient / curious observer approach (above)

**Email 2 (Day 3):** Case study / social proof

Subject: Results for a practice similar to yours

"Dr. [Name],

Following up on my last note. We recently worked with a chiropractor in [similar city] who was relying entirely on referrals. Within 90 days of launching Facebook ads + our follow-up system, they were consistently adding 20+ new patients/month.

Happy to share the breakdown if helpful. Would a 20-minute call make sense?

— [Name]"

**Email 3 (Day 7):** Problem-first, no pitch

Subject: Are you leaving new patients behind?

"Dr. [Name],

One thing we see constantly: practices spending $500–$1,500/month on random marketing (boosted posts, Yelp, etc.) and getting almost no results — because there's no system to capture and follow up on leads.

If that sounds familiar, I'd love to show you what the system looks like. If not, ignore this.

20-minute call?

— [Name]"

**Email 4 (Day 14):** Direct ask

Subject: Last email

"Dr. [Name],

I've sent a few notes your way — no worries if the timing isn't right. I'll stop filling your inbox.

If you ever want to look at how we get [chiropractors / dentists] consistent new patients, our calendar is at [link].

— [Name]"

**Email 5 (Day 30):** Seasonal or news-based reactivation

"Dr. [Name],

Quick follow-up — we just started working with a chiropractic group in [state] and have availability for one more [city] practice.

If new patient volume is on your radar, here's a 20-minute slot: [link]

— [Name]"

### Email Deliverability (Critical for Saleshandy Users)

**The problem:** Marketing emails from a new domain get filtered to spam. Halo uses `trygohalomarketing.com` for outreach.

**Best practices:**
- Warm the domain for 4 weeks before full volume sending (Saleshandy has warmup built-in — use it)
- Send from a real name, not info@ or marketing@ — use "Matt@trygohalomarketing.com" or "Preston@..."
- Keep sending volume under 100/day per mailbox initially; scale to 200–300 over 4 weeks
- Monitor: bounce rate < 3%, spam rate < 0.1%, open rate > 30% (if lower, fix subject lines)
- Personalize the first line — even just "I noticed [practice name] doesn't run Facebook ads" is enough to beat generic

## LinkedIn Outreach (Underused, High Quality)

LinkedIn is underused by most healthcare marketing agencies — which means less competition.

**Who to target on LinkedIn:**
- Practice owners / medical directors
- Office managers (they influence decisions)
- "Chiropractor" + self-employed filter
- Dentist + private practice filter

**Connection request note (150 char limit):**
> "Hi [Name], I work with [chiropractic / dental] practices on patient acquisition. Would love to connect."

**After connect — message 1:**
> "Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Quick question: are you actively looking to grow new patient volume, or is your schedule pretty full?"

**If they engage — message 2:**
> "Good to know. We run Facebook and Google campaigns for [chiropractors / dentists] and handle the full follow-up automation so leads actually convert. Worth a 20-minute call?"

## Tracking + CRM System

**Every lead goes into GHL immediately. No exceptions.**

For SDR:
- Log every call: outcome (no answer, voicemail, gatekeeper, spoke with prospect, booked call)
- Follow-up tasks created for every contact who didn't close on first touch
- Weekly report: calls made, conversations, discovery calls booked, closes (if on the call)

**Sequence management:**
- Use GHL or Saleshandy sequences for email (don't manually track follow-ups — human error)
- Cold call lists in GHL or a simple Google Sheet → mark status after every call
