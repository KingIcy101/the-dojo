## Google vs. Facebook — How They're Different

| | Facebook | Google |
|---|---|---|
| Intent | Creates demand | Captures demand |
| User mindset | Scrolling, passive | Actively searching |
| Lead quality | Lower (interrupted) | Higher (intent-based) |
| CPL | $15–$60 typically | $40–$200 typically |
| Time to results | Faster (algorithm learns in 7–14 days) | Fast (same day launch to first leads) |
| Best for | Awareness + education niches | High-intent, specific condition searches |
| Creative dependency | High (ads get stale fast) | Lower (keyword-driven, copy iterates slower) |

**Recommendation for Halo clients:** Run both. Google captures the people actively searching right now; Facebook builds a pipeline and creates appointments from people who weren't searching yet. Together they're 3–5x more effective than either alone.

## Google Ads Campaign Types for Healthcare

### 1. Search Campaigns (Core)

The bread and butter. Patient types "chiropractor near me" → your ad appears → they click → they book.

**Campaign structure:**

```
Campaign: [Practice Name] — Local Search
│
├─ Ad Group: Chiropractor Near Me
│   Keywords: [chiropractor near me], [chiropractor in {city}], [best chiropractor {city}]
│
├─ Ad Group: Back Pain
│   Keywords: [back pain doctor], [back pain treatment], [back pain relief {city}]
│
├─ Ad Group: Neck Pain / Headaches
│   Keywords: [neck pain relief], [cervical adjustment], [headache chiropractor]
│
├─ Ad Group: New Patient Specific
│   Keywords: [new patient chiropractor], [affordable chiropractor], [chiro accepting new patients]
│
└─ Ad Group: Competitor Brand
    Keywords: [competitor name chiropractor] (if budget allows)
```

**Match types:**
- Phrase match: "chiropractor near me" — moderate control, good reach
- Exact match: [chiropractor {city}] — tight control, lower volume
- Broad match with Smart Bidding: Use carefully, can waste budget fast without proper negatives

**Negative keywords (always exclude):**
- chiropractic school, chiropractic college, chiropractic degree (job seekers, students)
- free chiropractor (unless you're advertising a free offer)
- chiropractor salary (HR/job related)
- what does a chiropractor do (too informational, not ready to book)

### 2. Local Services Ads (LSAs) — PRIORITY

Google LSAs are pay-per-lead (not pay-per-click) and show above standard ads with a "Google Guaranteed" badge. They're especially powerful for:
- Chiropractors
- Dentists
- Physical therapists
- Home services adjacent (Halo can help clients set these up)

**Why LSAs matter:**
- Google Guaranteed badge dramatically increases trust
- Pay per verified lead, not per click (less waste)
- Show up in top position for "near me" searches
- Usually $25–$80 per lead, depending on competition

**Setup requirements:** Google verifies the practice (background check, license, insurance). Halo can walk clients through this process — it's a value-add service.

### 3. Performance Max (PMax)

Google's automated, AI-driven campaign type that serves across Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, Shopping. Uses goals + asset groups to find conversions anywhere on Google's network.

**When to use for healthcare:**
- After you have conversion data (50+ conversions in 30 days) — PMax learns from this
- For practices with bigger budgets ($3K+/month ad spend) where manual campaigns have hit ceiling
- Good for telehealth (national reach) — not ideal for hyper-local practices where you want geographic control

**Warning:** PMax can burn budget on irrelevant placements without proper setup. Requires asset groups with strong creative and clear conversion goals.

### 4. Display / Remarketing

Target people who visited the website or landing page but didn't convert.

- **Remarketing window:** 30 days for general healthcare (shorter is better — ad fatigue)
- **Creative:** Patient testimonials, offer reminders, "Still looking for a chiropractor?"
- **Placement:** Exclude adult content, gambling, and irrelevant sites (the default placements are often terrible)
- **Budget:** 10–15% of total Google budget is reasonable

## Keyword Strategy by Niche

### Chiropractic — Top Keywords

**High intent (most valuable):**
- chiropractor near me
- chiropractor [city name]
- back pain chiropractor
- neck pain treatment [city]
- same day chiropractor appointment

**Medium intent (good volume, worth targeting):**
- back pain relief
- chiropractic adjustment
- spinal decompression [city]
- sciatica treatment [city]

**Low intent / informational (use as negatives or separate campaign with different bid):**
- what is chiropractic
- how does chiropractic work
- is chiropractic safe

**Average CPCs (competitive markets):** $3–$12/click for general; $15–$30 for "near me" in dense markets

### Dental — Top Keywords

**High intent:**
- dentist near me
- dentist accepting new patients [city]
- emergency dentist [city]
- dental implants [city]
- teeth whitening [city]
- cosmetic dentist [city]
- family dentist [city]

**High value but expensive:**
- dental implants (CPCs can reach $30–$80 in major cities)
- all on 4 dental implants
- invisalign [city]

**Pro tip:** Emergency dental is gold. Someone with tooth pain RIGHT NOW converts at extremely high rates. Separate campaign, higher bids, 24/7 ad scheduling.

### Telehealth — Top Keywords

**Highly variable by specialty. Examples:**

Weight loss telehealth:
- online weight loss doctor
- GLP-1 prescription online
- semaglutide telehealth

Mental health:
- online therapist
- therapy near me (yes, people search this even for telehealth)
- anxiety medication online

General telehealth:
- online doctor [condition]
- telehealth appointment
- virtual care [specialty]

## Google Ad Copy — What Works

### Responsive Search Ads (RSA) Structure

Google requires 15 headlines and 4 descriptions. The AI mixes and matches. Write them so any combination makes sense.

**Headline types to include:**
1. Brand: "Halo Chiropractic [City]"
2. Keyword-match: "Chiropractor in [City]"
3. Benefit: "Same-Day Appointments Available"
4. Social proof: "4.9 Stars · 300+ Reviews"
5. Offer: "New Patient Exam $49"
6. Urgency: "Only 5 New Patient Slots Left"
7. Pain: "Back Pain? Find Relief Today"
8. Trust: "Accepting New Patients"

**Description types to include:**
1. Problem + solution: "Tired of living with chronic back pain? Our team specializes in natural, drug-free relief. Book today."
2. Process: "Book online in 60 seconds. Same-day appointments often available. Most insurance accepted."
3. Social proof: "Join 500+ patients who've found relief at [Practice]. 4.9 stars on Google."
4. Offer: "New patients get a comprehensive exam + x-rays for $49. Call or book online now."

### Ad Extensions (Always Use)

- **Call extension:** Practice phone number. Patients call directly from the ad. Enable call recording in Google Ads.
- **Location extension:** Links to Google Business Profile. Shows address + map pin.
- **Sitelink extensions:** "Book Online," "Our Services," "Meet the Team," "Patient Reviews"
- **Callout extensions:** "Same-Day Appointments," "Insurance Accepted," "New Patients Welcome," "Free Parking"
- **Structured snippet extensions:** Services: Chiropractic, Massage, Physical Therapy, etc.

## Bidding Strategy

**When starting (no conversion data):**
- Manual CPC or Enhanced CPC — you control spend, learn before automating
- Set bids by keyword group based on CPC estimates from Keyword Planner

**After 30+ conversions in 30 days:**
- Switch to Target CPA (tCPA) — tell Google what you want to pay per lead
- Set tCPA 20–30% above your actual target to give the algorithm room to learn
- Do NOT switch bidding strategies more than once every 2 weeks (resets learning)

**After 50+ conversions in 30 days:**
- Maximize Conversion Value if you know LTV by lead type
- Or continue tCPA with confidence

## Google Business Profile — Underrated Channel

For hyper-local practices, Google Business Profile (GBP) is often the highest-converting channel — and it's free. Halo should optimize every client's GBP as part of onboarding.

**GBP Optimization Checklist:**
- [ ] All categories correct (Primary: Chiropractor, Additional: Physical therapy, etc.)
- [ ] Hours accurate and updated (including holiday hours)
- [ ] Photos: interior, exterior, team, before/after (for cosmetic), 20+ photos
- [ ] Posts: weekly (announcement, offer, tip, event)
- [ ] Review response: reply to all reviews within 48 hours
- [ ] Q&A: seed with common questions and answer them yourself
- [ ] Services list: fully populated with descriptions and prices
- [ ] Messaging enabled
- [ ] Booking link: connected to GHL calendar

**Reviews strategy:** The practice with the most (and best) reviews wins local pack placement. Halo should automate review requests: GHL sends SMS after visit → links to Google review page. Even 10 new reviews/month compounds fast.

| Monthly Ad Budget | Google Allocation | Facebook Allocation |
|---|---|---|
| $1,000 | 60% ($600) | 40% ($400) |
| $2,000 | 50% ($1,000) | 50% ($1,000) |

**Logic:** At lower budgets, Google captures existing demand more efficiently. At higher budgets, Facebook's scale and lower CPL make it the growth driver while Google handles intent.

## The Google Analytics Setup Halo Must Do For Every Client

Without proper conversion tracking, you're flying blind.

**What to track:**
1. Form submission on landing page (primary conversion)
2. Phone call from ad (using Google Call Forwarding or CallRail)
3. Call from website (track as separate conversion)
4. Appointment booking confirmation page
5. Chat initiation (if applicable)

**Tools needed:**
- Google Tag Manager (GTM) — installs all tracking without touching website code
- Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — audience insights, user behavior
- Google Ads Conversion Tracking — direct from GTM
- GHL native tracking + UTM parameters on all ad URLs

**UTM structure for Halo:**
```
utm_source=google
utm_medium=cpc
utm_campaign=[practice-name]-[campaign-type]
utm_content=[ad-group-name]
utm_term={keyword}
```

This lets Halo show clients exactly which keywords drove which leads — a massive reporting differentiator.
