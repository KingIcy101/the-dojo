_The operational backbone that keeps Halo's clients paying month after month._
_Research by Halo. 2026-02-21._

---

## Why Onboarding Is Your Most Important Sales Call

The moment a client signs is the most optimistic they'll ever feel. Your job in the first 30 days is to:
1. Validate their decision (make them feel smart for hiring you)
2. Set expectations correctly (don't overpromise, then underdeliver)
3. Get early wins on the board (even small ones compound trust fast)

Agencies that lose clients in month 1–3 almost always fail here. Either they set wrong expectations, moved too slow, or didn't communicate proactively.

**Halo's target:** Ads live within 5 business days of signed contract. First leads within 7–10 days.

## The Onboarding Process (Step-by-Step)

### Day 0 — Contract Signed

- [ ] Send welcome email: "You're officially a Halo client — here's what happens next"
- [ ] Add to Slack (or WhatsApp group for practice) — their direct line to the team
- [ ] Create GHL sub-account (use niche template: chiro/dental/telehealth)
- [ ] Send onboarding questionnaire (see below)
- [ ] Calendar the kickoff call within 48 hours

### Day 1–2 — Kickoff Call (45–60 min)

**Goal:** Get everything needed to launch. Leave with zero blockers.

**Agenda:**
1. Introductions (5 min) — who they'll work with, how communication flows
2. Business deep-dive (15 min) — their best patients, biggest pain points, what makes them different
3. Offer confirmation (10 min) — what are we promoting? ($49 exam? Free screening? Insurance?)
4. Access collection (10 min) — get all logins and credentials
5. Creative brief (10 min) — photos/videos they have, doctor's comfort with being on camera
6. Timeline walkthrough (5 min) — "here's exactly when you'll see your first lead"
7. Q&A (5 min)

**Accesses to collect on kickoff call:**
- Facebook Business Manager (add as Admin)
- Google Ads account (create new or get access)
- Google Analytics (if existing)
- Google Business Profile (add as Manager)
- Website access (for pixel installation)
- Practice management / booking system (to integrate with GHL calendar)
- Photos + logos (request via Google Drive link)

**Onboarding questionnaire (send before kickoff):**
```
1. What's your most common patient complaint/condition?
2. What's your best offer for new patients? (discounted exam, free consult, etc.)
3. What does your average patient spend in the first 3–6 months?
4. What geographic area do you serve? (radius from practice)
5. Who is your ideal patient? (age, gender, condition, lifestyle)
6. What makes your practice different from others nearby?
7. Do you have any patient testimonials or before/afters we can use?
8. Are you comfortable appearing in video ads?
9. What's your current booking system?
10. Any previous agency experience? What went well/poorly?
```

### Day 2–3 — Foundation Setup

- [ ] GHL sub-account fully configured (pipeline, automations, calendar)
- [ ] Landing page built from niche template, customized with client brand/photos
- [ ] Facebook Pixel installed on landing page
- [ ] Google Tag Manager installed (if using Google Ads)
- [ ] Bland.ai call script written for this practice
- [ ] 7-day SMS/email follow-up sequence loaded in GHL
- [ ] Appointment reminder sequence loaded

### Day 3–5 — Campaigns Launch

- [ ] Facebook campaign created (cold + retargeting structure)
- [ ] Google campaign created (if included in package)
- [ ] Ads reviewed internally before going live (copy, targeting, landing page check)
- [ ] Test lead submitted to verify automation fires correctly
- [ ] Client notified: "Your ads are live — expect first leads within 48–72 hours"

### Day 7–10 — First Leads

- [ ] Monitor early performance daily (CTR, CPL, any issues)
- [ ] Send client update: "Here are your first X leads — here's what to expect as the algorithm optimizes"
- [ ] Confirm automations are firing (call the lead number from a test submission)
- [ ] Address any issues immediately (budget, targeting, landing page conversion)

## The 30-60-90 Day Check-in Structure

### 30-Day Check-in (Critical)

The 30-day call is your first real retention checkpoint. If a client is going to churn, it usually happens around day 45–60. Get ahead of it.

**Agenda:**
1. Results review: leads generated, calls booked, appointments showed
2. Campaign performance: CPL, CTR, best-performing ads
3. Feedback: What's the practice team saying? Are leads quality? Any friction in the booking process?
4. Optimizations done or planned
5. Month 2 goals set

**What to have ready:**
- GHL pipeline report (leads → booked → showed)
- Facebook/Google campaign screenshot with key metrics
- At least 1 win to highlight (even "we optimized X and CPL dropped 20%")

**The most important question to ask:**
> "On a scale of 1–10, how satisfied are you with the results so far — and what would make it a 10?"

This surfaces issues before they become churn.

### 60-Day Check-in

- Results are compounding by now (algorithm has data, follow-up is running)
- First real case study data should be forming
- Surface the ROI calculation: "You've spent $X total and gotten Y new patients — at your average patient value of $Z, that's a [X]x return"
- If performing well: plant the seed for adding a second platform or service
- If struggling: problem-solve openly, show them what's changing, give them a clear recovery timeline

### 90-Day Review (QBR)

- Formal business review: last 90 days, numbers, wins, challenges
- 12-month roadmap: where could this go if we scale?
- Lock in the next 90 days of goals
- If results are strong: this is the right moment to propose an annual contract or upsell
- This call prevents churn more than any other single action

## Managing Client Expectations (Critical for Retention)

The #1 cause of churn that isn't performance-related: **wrong expectations set at the sale.**

### What to promise:
- "Ads live within 5 business days"
- "Your first leads within 7–10 days of launch"
- "The algorithm typically optimizes over 4–6 weeks — month 2 and 3 are usually significantly better than month 1"
- "We'll communicate every week — you'll never wonder what's happening"

### What NOT to promise:
- A specific number of new patients in month 1
- A specific CPL before the campaign has data
- That every lead will book (they won't — your job is the follow-up system, not a guarantee)

### The "ugly first month" conversation:
Have this on the kickoff call, before month 1 starts:
> "I want to set you up for success by being honest about the first month. Facebook's algorithm needs 7–14 days of data to optimize. CPL is often highest in week 1–2, then drops significantly. Month 2 and 3 are almost always our best months. So if month 1 looks slower than you hoped — that's normal, not a problem."

This one conversation prevents most month-1 churn conversations.

## The Retention Scorecard

Track these monthly for every client. Flag any that score below target.

| Metric | Red (at risk) | Yellow (watch) | Green (healthy) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leads this month | < 5 | 5–10 | > 10 |
| Lead → booked rate | < 15% | 15–25% | > 25% |
| Client satisfaction (1–10) | < 6 | 6–7 | > 8 |
| Days since last client call | > 30 | 14–30 | < 14 |
| Report delivered on time | No | N/A | Yes |
| Open Slack messages | > 48hr | 24–48hr | < 24hr |

Any client with 2+ red flags needs immediate attention from Matt/Preston, not the AM.

## Turning Happy Clients into Growth

### The referral ask (month 3–4):

Once a client is happy and seeing results:
> "Hey Dr. [Name] — we're expanding into more [chiropractic / dental] practices in Virginia. Do you know any practitioners who might be struggling to get consistent new patients? If you refer someone who becomes a client, we'll credit your next month's fee."

Healthcare practitioners talk constantly — at CE events, through referral networks, at practice management conferences. One referral from a happy client closes faster than 50 cold calls.

### The upsell sequence:

**Month 3:** "We're seeing strong results on Facebook. Adding Google would capture the people actively searching right now — we typically see a 40–60% lift in total leads when both platforms are running."

**Month 4:** "Your show rate is great but I want to make sure no-shows don't become a problem as volume grows. We could add a review-generation sequence — you've helped 30+ patients this quarter, imagine if half of them left a Google review."

**Month 6:** "You're at [X] new patients per month. If we doubled that — what would that mean for your practice? We could explore scaling the ad budget or adding a second niche campaign."

## Firing a Client (Yes, Sometimes)

Signs it's time to end the relationship:
- Constant complaint calls despite solid metrics
- Makes changes to ads/landing pages without approval, then blames agency
- Hasn't followed through on their side (Bland.ai won't pick up because practice won't answer calls to confirm the number)
- Consistently late paying
- Verbally abusive to team members

**How to off-board gracefully:**
> "After reviewing our last few months together, I think we've reached the limits of what this engagement can produce in its current form. I want to be honest with you rather than take your money indefinitely. I'm going to refund [X] and hand off everything we've built — your landing page, your automations, all the campaign data — so you have a solid foundation regardless of what you do next."

This protects Halo's reputation and sometimes turns a bad client into a referral source (because you were honest with them when you didn't have to be).
