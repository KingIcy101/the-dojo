## Why Facebook Works for Healthcare

Facebook/Instagram is the #1 patient acquisition channel for local practices because:
- **Demographics match:** 35–65 year olds (the people with chronic pain, dental needs, health concerns) use Facebook actively
- **Visual storytelling:** Before/afters, patient testimonials, educational content drive trust
- **Local targeting:** Radius targeting + demographic layering finds patients in the practice's service area
- **Intent creation (not capture):** Google captures demand. Facebook creates it. People aren't searching for a chiropractor — Halo shows them why they need one.
- **Cost per lead:** Healthcare FB leads typically $15–$60 CPL (vs. $50–$200 for Google), depending on niche

## HIPAA Compliance — Critical Rules

**This is non-negotiable.** Getting this wrong can blow up a client's practice.

### What NOT to Do
- Don't use Custom Audiences built from patient data (email lists from EHR = HIPAA violation)
- Don't pixel a thank-you page that reveals someone is a patient (can be used to create lookalikes from patient data)
- Don't use health information in retargeting (e.g., retargeting people who visited a "knee pain" page as a "knee pain patient")
- Don't run ads that imply a specific person has a health condition

### What's Allowed
- Targeting by demographics, geography, interests (not PHI)
- Interest-based targeting: "Health & Wellness," "Fitness," "Physical Therapy"
- Behavioral targeting: people who recently moved (new area, new provider need)
- Lookalike audiences built from LEAD data (not patient data) — leads have consented
- Pixel tracking on pre-conversion pages (landing page visits, but not post-appointment confirmation)
- General healthcare interest targeting

### Best Practice for Halo
- All clients should sign a BAA with Meta before running health-related ads (Meta offers this)
- Keep ad copy compliant: "Are you dealing with back pain?" ✅ vs. "We know you have back pain" ❌
- Landing pages: use Halo-managed GHL pages, not the practice's own site (controls the data environment)
- Lead gen forms: use Facebook Lead Ads forms OR GHL landing pages (not embedded practice forms)

## Campaign Structure (What Actually Works)

### Account Structure
```
Campaign Level: Objective = Leads (or Conversions)
  ↓
Ad Set Level: Audience + Placement + Budget
  ↓
Ad Level: Creative + Copy + CTA
```

### Recommended Campaign Architecture for a Single Practice

**Campaign 1: Cold Audience — Lead Generation**
- Objective: Lead generation
- Ad Set 1A: Local awareness (radius 5–15mi, demographics 30–65, broad interests)
- Ad Set 1B: Health-interested (radius, + interests: chiropractic, physical therapy, wellness)
- Ad Set 1C: Behavior-based (recent movers, family parents, home ownership)

**Campaign 2: Warm Audience — Retargeting**
- Objective: Lead generation or Conversions
- Ad Set 2A: Website visitors (last 30 days)
- Ad Set 2B: Video viewers (50%+ view of educational video)
- Ad Set 2C: Lead form openers who didn't submit

**Campaign 3: Lookalike — Scale**
- Objective: Lead generation
- Ad Set 3A: 1-2% LAL from existing leads
- Ad Set 3B: 2-5% LAL for broader reach

### Budget Philosophy
- New client launch: $20–$40/day ($600–$1,200/month ad spend)
- Scale once CPL < $40 and lead → booked rate > 20%
- Max scale without creative refresh: usually 2x in 2 weeks without shocking the algorithm
- Monthly spend per client: recommend $1,000–$3,000 for most practices

## What Ads Actually Work (Creative Strategy)

### #1 Hook Types That Convert in Healthcare

**Pain-based hooks** (highest CTR, most emotional):
- "Still waking up with back pain every morning?"
- "Tired of living with chronic knee pain?"
- "3 signs your headaches might be coming from your neck"
- "Why most dentists miss this in your first exam"

**Social proof hooks:**
- "Over 200 [City] families trust us for their dental care"
- "[Patient name] came in barely able to walk. Here's what happened in 6 weeks."
- "4.9 stars, 300+ reviews — here's what patients say"

**Curiosity/education hooks:**
- "The #1 thing most chiropractors DON'T tell you about back pain"
- "This simple test predicts 80% of dental issues before they become expensive"
- "Why telehealth patients get better results than in-office — a provider explains"

**Offer-based hooks:**
- "$59 New Patient Exam + X-Rays (Regularly $250)"
- "Free spinal health screening — this week only"
- "Book your new patient consultation — we have 6 slots this month"

### Best Ad Formats by Stage

| Funnel Stage | Format | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cold (awareness) | Video (15–60s) | Educates, builds trust before asking for anything |
| Cold (lead gen) | Single image with strong hook | Fast, cheap, tests quickly |
| Warm (retargeting) | Carousel (social proof) | Shows depth, testimonials, multiple reasons to book |
| Warm (retargeting) | Short video testimonial | Patient story — most powerful conversion tool |
| All stages | Lead form vs. landing page | Test both; LPs usually higher quality, forms higher volume |

### Video Ad Formula (60 seconds)
```
0:00–0:05  Hook — "If you're dealing with [specific pain]..."
0:05–0:20  Problem agitation — "Most people think [wrong belief] but actually..."
0:20–0:40  Solution — "At [Practice Name], we use [approach] to [result]"
0:40–0:55  Social proof — "[patient name] came in with X, now they're doing Y"
0:55–1:00  CTA — "Click below to schedule your free consultation"
```

### Image Ad Formula
- Headline: Pain/problem statement or direct benefit
- Primary text: 2–3 sentences max. Hook → proof → CTA.
- Image: Real patient (with consent), doctor in action, before/after (dental/chiro), or high-quality branded graphic
- CTA button: "Book Now," "Get Started," or "Learn More" (test all three)

## Lead Qualification System

**The Problem:** Facebook generates leads, but most aren't ready to book. Without follow-up, 60–80% of leads go cold.

**Halo's Solution (using GHL + Bland.ai):**

1. **Lead submits form** → GHL immediately captures, tags "facebook-lead"
2. **Within 2 minutes:** Automated SMS fires → "Hi [name], thanks for reaching out to [Practice]! Our team will call you shortly to find a time that works."
3. **Within 5 minutes:** Bland.ai call → AI positions as scheduling team → "Are you still looking to schedule?" → Books appointment or captures objection
4. **If no answer:** Follow-up sequence over 7 days (SMS + email, 5–6 touches)
5. **If booked:** Appointment reminder sequence (day before + 2 hours before, SMS)
6. **GHL tracks:** Lead source → booked → showed → treated. This is the ROI proof.

**Why this matters:** Practices that call leads within 5 minutes convert at 5–10x the rate vs. calling after 30 minutes. The Bland.ai system makes this instant and scalable.

## Facebook Ad Targeting — Deep Dive by Niche

### Chiropractic
- **Primary interests:** Back pain, chiropractic care, physical therapy, yoga, running, sports
- **Demographics:** 28–65, any gender (skew female for family, male for sports injury)
- **Behaviors:** Parents of young children (posture issues), athletes (sports injuries), desk workers (neck pain)
- **Location:** 5–10 mile radius max (chiro is hyper-local)
- **Best offer:** Free consultation OR discounted new patient exam ($49–$99)

### Dental
- **Primary interests:** Dental care, oral health, cosmetic dentistry, teeth whitening
- **Demographics:** 25–60 — broad (everyone has teeth)
- **Behaviors:** Recent movers (new area, need new dentist), parents with young children (family dentist), engagement triggers (wedding = cosmetic)
- **Location:** 5–15 mile radius (people drive further for dental)
- **Best offers:** Free whitening with new patient exam, $0 first cleaning with x-rays, cosmetic consultation

### Telehealth
- **Challenge:** No geographic constraint is a double-edged sword — can target nationally but must pick a strategy
- **Primary interests:** Mental health, chronic pain, weight loss, diabetes management (depending on specialty)
- **Demographics:** Varies heavily by condition — match demographics to patient profile
- **Behaviors:** Previously engaged with health content, streaming service users (tech-comfortable), mobile-first users
- **Best offers:** Free first consultation, $49 initial appointment, condition-specific landing pages
- **Note:** Telehealth FB ads are trickier due to Meta's "Special Ad Categories" for health. Often need to run as "Employment" or without detailed health targeting. Test carefully.

## Key Metrics and Benchmarks

| Metric | Poor | Good | Great |
|---|---|---|---|
| CTR (click-through rate) | < 0.8% | 1–2% | > 2.5% |
| CPL (cost per lead) | > $80 | $25–$50 | < $20 |
| Lead → Booked rate | < 10% | 20–35% | > 40% |
| Booked → Show rate | < 40% | 60–75% | > 80% |
| CPAcquisition (new patient) | > $400 | $100–$250 | < $100 |
| ROAS (for practices) | < 3x | 5–10x | > 15x |

**Key insight:** The practice's lifetime value of a patient determines how much to spend. A chiropractor whose average patient is worth $1,200 LTV can afford $200 per acquired patient. A dental practice with a $3,000 avg patient LTV can afford $500+. Frame this in your sales conversation — tie CPA to LTV, not just ad spend.

## Reporting — What to Show Clients

Clients don't understand CPM, CTR, ROAS. They understand:
1. **How many leads did I get this month?** (volume)
2. **How many booked appointments?** (conversion)
3. **How many actually showed up?** (show rate)
4. **How much did I spend, and what's the return?** (ROI)

GHL tracks this if set up correctly. Monthly reports should show:
- Leads generated (Facebook + Google separately)
- Leads → booked conversion rate
- Appointments showed
- Revenue attributed (if practice tracks this)
- Month-over-month trend

**The retention play:** If the client can see that for every $1K in ad spend they got 3 booked patients who each paid $500, that's a 150% ROI. Nobody cancels that.
