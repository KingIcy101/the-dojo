## 🦴 CHIROPRACTIC — The Core Niche

Chiro is Halo's home base (Renee is a chiropractor). It's also the best niche to start in:
- High patient pain = high emotional urgency in ads
- Low price resistance (most people can afford $75/visit)
- Fast sales cycle (they know they need marketing, they just don't trust agencies yet)
- Many solo/small practice owners — fast decisions, no committee

### Who the Patient Is

**Primary demographics:**
- Age 28–65, slight female skew (especially for family care and pediatric chiro)
- Has had a specific injury (car accident, sports, labor work) OR chronic condition (back pain, neck pain, headaches, sciatica)
- Often tried home remedies, OTC pain meds, maybe physical therapy before seeking chiro
- Has insurance but is sometimes cash-pay for chiro (not always covered)

**The emotional journey:**
1. Living with pain, trying to ignore it
2. Pain disrupts daily life (can't sleep, can't work out, can't pick up kids)
3. Searches for solution → Facebook shows an ad → lands on page → submits
4. Skeptical (has tried other things, worried chiro won't work)
5. Wants: relief, not just information. Fast results. Doctor who listens.

### What Ads Work for Chiro

**Highest-performing ad angles:**

1. **The breakthrough story:**
"[Patient name] came in barely able to get out of bed. After 6 visits, she was back to hiking with her family. Here's what we found — and what we did."

2. **The specific condition:**
"If you have sciatica, the pain in your leg isn't actually coming from your leg. Here's what's really happening — and how we treat it without drugs or surgery."

3. **The limited offer:**
"We're accepting 8 new patients this month. New patient complete exam + X-rays: $49 (regularly $250). Available this week only."

4. **The credibility builder:**
"300+ Google reviews. 4.9 stars. [City]'s most trusted chiropractor since 2012. Ready to feel the difference?"

5. **The myth-buster:**
"Think chiropractic is 'cracking your back'? Here's what a real spinal assessment actually reveals — and why most back pain comes from something most people never check."

### Chiro Landing Page Must-Haves
- Specific condition in headline (not generic "back pain" — say "lower back pain" or "sciatica")
- Doctor's photo (builds trust fast)
- "Before we'll even charge you" framing on the exam offer
- Patient testimonial video (even 30 seconds, recorded on an iPhone, is gold)
- Insurance statement: "We accept most major insurance plans"
- Address + Google Maps embed (local trust signal)

### Chiro Onboarding Intelligence (What to Ask)

On the kickoff call, always gather:
- What's their most common patient complaint? (back, neck, sciatica, headache)
- What's the offer they're willing to run? (Can they do a $49 new patient? Free screening?)
- What does their average patient spend in 3–6 months?
- What's their current booking method? (Call in, Zocdoc, Jane App, GHL, manual?)
- Do they have any video content? (patient testimonials, walk-through, doctor intro)
- What other practitioners refer patients to them? (could be partnership targets)

## 🦷 DENTAL — The Volume Play

Dental is a massive market with extremely predictable patient behavior. Everyone needs a dentist. The challenge: it's competitive, so you need to target well.

### Dental Patient Segments (Pick One Per Campaign)

**Segment 1: The "Just Need a Dentist" patient**
- Recently moved, doesn't have a dentist, needs cleaning + checkup
- Message: Accepting new patients, easy booking, insurance accepted, friendly team
- Targeting: Recent movers, people who haven't booked dental in 12+ months (Facebook "engaged with dental content")

**Segment 2: The Cosmetic patient**
- Wants whiter teeth, straighter teeth, veneers, implants
- Message: Transformation, smile, confidence, photos of results
- LTV: 3–5x higher than general dental. Worth spending more to acquire.
- Targeting: 25–45, engaged/married (wedding prep), white collar professionals (appearance-conscious)

**Segment 3: The Emergency patient**
- Tooth pain RIGHT NOW — highest intent, fastest conversion
- Message: "Emergency dental — same day appointments available"
- Targeting: Anyone within 10–15 miles (emergency isn't selective)
- Platform: Google Search (highest intent for this segment — they're Googling right now)

**Segment 4: The Parent / Family patient**
- Has kids, looking for a family dentist
- Message: "We make kids love the dentist," family-friendly, TV in every room, etc.
- Targeting: Parents of young children (Facebook behavioral targeting)

### What Converts in Dental Ads

**Best offer:**
- Free whitening with new patient exam (perceived high value, low cost to practice)
- $0 new patient cleaning + X-rays with insurance
- "No insurance? No problem" — flat-fee new patient exam for uninsured ($79–$149)

**Strongest social proof:**
- Patient smile transformations (before/after)
- Specific reviews about pain-free experience ("I was terrified of dentists until I found...")
- Children's reviews (from parents)

### Dental-Specific Compliance Notes
- Can show before/after for cosmetic procedures (unlike some medical specialties)
- Patient photo consent is essential — always get signed release
- Don't claim specific outcomes ("guaranteed") — "most patients see" or "many patients experience"

### Dental Pricing at Halo
- Standard: $1,950/month (general dental, 1 platform)
- Cosmetic dental: $2,500–$2,950/month (higher LTV justifies more spend on qualified leads)
- Multi-location DSO: Custom (enterprise tier, not Halo's current focus)

## 💻 TELEHEALTH — The Growth Frontier

Telehealth exploded post-COVID and hasn't retracted. It's now a permanent channel for patient care and one of the highest-growth healthcare segments.

### Why Telehealth is Different (And Harder)

1. **No geography constraint** — both a blessing and a problem. Where do you target?
2. **Higher skepticism** — patients still prefer in-person for many things. Ads need to overcome this.
3. **Regulation complexity** — telehealth is regulated by state medical boards. Can't always advertise cross-state services.
4. **Condition specificity** — telehealth works best for chronic conditions, mental health, weight management, not acute/emergency care
5. **Insurance complexity** — telehealth reimbursement varies by state, payer, and condition. Affects conversion.

### Best Telehealth Niches for Halo (Highest Conversion)

**Weight loss / GLP-1 / Semaglutide:**
- Huge demand right now (Ozempic / Wegovy boom)
- FB CPL: $20–$60
- Strong emotional angle: transformation, health, confidence
- Note: Meta has restrictions on before/after for weight loss — work around with testimonials, lifestyle imagery

**Mental health (therapy, psychiatry, medication management):**
- High demand, high LTV (ongoing monthly sessions)
- Emotional hooks: "You don't have to feel this way," destigmatization angles
- Note: More sensitive targeting required (can't target by "depression" interests on Meta)

**Brain injury / TBI / neurological (Mike/Andy's niche):**
- Specialized, smaller audience
- High LTV per patient
- Ad strategy: educational (most patients don't know telehealth options exist for this)
- Target: caregivers of patients, not just patients themselves (caregivers often make the appointment)

**Hormone therapy / testosterone / HRT:**
- Growing fast, men's and women's health
- Strong pain-point messaging: energy, weight, libido, vitality
- Typically not fully covered by insurance — cash-pay market = higher urgency to convert

**Primary care / urgent care (virtual):**
- High frequency (ongoing relationship)
- Typically price-competitive market
- Best angle: convenience ("See a doctor in 20 minutes, no waiting room")

### Telehealth Ad Strategy

**Facebook targeting for telehealth:**
- Interest-based: condition-adjacent (not condition-specific — HIPAA edge)
- Behavioral: engaged with health content, health & wellness enthusiasts, mobile device heavy users
- Demographic: match to condition (e.g., 35–65 women for hormone therapy, 25–50 men for testosterone)
- National or regional targeting — match to where the practice is licensed to practice

**Google targeting:**
- Search intent is high: "[condition] telehealth," "online doctor for [condition]," "virtual [specialty]"
- Use location targeting: target states where the practice is licensed

**Landing page angle:**
- Lead with convenience: "See a doctor without leaving home"
- Address skepticism: "Board-certified physicians, not bots"
- HIPAA prominently: "Fully HIPAA-compliant platform"
- Insurance: "We accept [payers] or see self-pay options from $[price]"
- Speed CTA: "Book your appointment in 2 minutes"

### Telehealth Pricing at Halo
- Starting at $2,950/month (higher complexity justifies it)
- National campaigns may require larger ad budgets ($2K–$5K/month ad spend)
- High LTV practices (ongoing chronic care) can justify $5K+ monthly management fees at scale

## 💆 MED SPA — The High-Margin Opportunity

Not Halo's current focus but worth knowing for expansion.

### Why Med Spas Work for Agencies
- Clients are cash-pay (no insurance complexity)
- High LTV ($3,000–$15,000 per regular client/year)
- Visual services = great for video and before/after content
- Impulse-buy friendly (Botox, fillers, laser — not scary procedures)
- Strong social proof culture (patients share results on Instagram)

### Ad Angles for Med Spas
- Before/after (allowed for cosmetic procedures — get consent)
- Limited-time offers: "3 units of Botox free with your first appointment this month"
- Educational: "What actually happens to your face when you stop doing Botox? A nurse explains."
- Seasonal: Valentine's Day, wedding season, summer bikini season, holiday party season

### Pricing for Med Spa Clients
- $2,500–$4,000/month (they can afford it, LTV justifies it)

## Referral Networks Between Niches

One thing most agencies miss: healthcare providers refer patients to each other constantly. A well-placed referral partnership can be worth more than any ad.

**For Halo's chiro clients:**
- Build relationships with: physical therapists, orthopedic surgeons, primary care physicians, pain management doctors, massage therapists, personal trainers
- Strategy: Help clients set up a formal referral program for other providers (not patient referrals — provider referrals)

**For Halo's dental clients:**
- Build relationships with: orthodontists, oral surgeons, ENTs, pediatricians
- Referral play: "Our GP refers all his patients who need implants to Dr. Smith's practice"

**For Halo's telehealth clients:**
- Partner with: primary care physicians who don't offer the specialty, hospital discharge coordinators, insurance navigators

**For Halo itself:**
- One happy chiro client → they mention Halo to their dental colleague who's also frustrated with marketing
- A satisfied client is a warmer referral than any cold call
- Build a formal referral incentive: $500 credit for a referring client who converts and stays 60+ days
