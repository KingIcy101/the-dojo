## The Profiles Worth Studying

### Jordan Platten — UK-based SMMA educator, scaled to $50K+/month
**Key lessons:**
- Niched into local businesses first, then productized hard
- Built his agency WHILE creating YouTube content about it — the content became his best client acquisition tool
- Emphasized: "Your agency is not your niche's marketing agency — it's YOUR agency. Your personality and trust IS the brand."
- Stressed daily outreach volume above everything else in year 1
- Rejected the "passive income" framing — real agencies require active sales until you have a referral flywheel

**Most applicable to Halo:** His cold outreach systems and discovery call framework are battle-tested. His content strategy (documenting the journey) could work for Halo's own brand.

### Iman Gadzhi — Grew agency from 0 to $1M/year by age 19
**Key lessons:**
- Started with social media management ($500/month), raised prices as proof accumulated
- The #1 mistake he cites: taking too many clients too fast without systems → delivery collapsed
- His fix: **Systematize before you scale.** Document every process. Build templates. Then add clients.
- Pricing philosophy: "Charge what makes the client take you seriously." He went from $500 → $2,500 → $5,000 as his confidence and proof grew.
- Built an agency education brand (SMMA blueprint) on the back of his agency — not replicable at Halo's stage, but worth knowing

**Most applicable to Halo:** The delivery-before-scale lesson. Every new client Halo takes on should be handled with a documented process, not improvised.

**Key lessons:**
- Built specifically in the healthcare niche — closest direct comparison to Halo

### Sabri Suby — King Kong agency, Australia, $1B+ in client revenue generated
**Key lessons:**
- Built the most profitable agency in Australia by obsessing over one thing: **client ROI**
- Famous line: "The agency with the best funnel wins, not the best service."
- His agency markets itself relentlessly — King Kong runs ads for King Kong
- Preaches "Sell Like Crazy" methodology: hook → story → offer, with extreme focus on direct response
- Believes all marketing comes down to: right message, right person, right time

### Alex Hormozi — Gym Launch → $100M Offers
**Key lessons:**
- Built a gym consulting business by going ALL-IN on one niche (gyms) and creating a product so good clients couldn't say no
- His "$100M Offers" framework applies directly to Halo's offer construction:
  - **Dream outcome:** 15–25 new patients/month consistently
  - **Perceived likelihood of success:** Case studies, guarantee elements, proof
  - **Time to result:** "Ads live in 5 days, first leads within 7 days"
  - **Effort required from client:** "We handle everything — you just show up and treat patients"
- The formula: Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)
- Increase the top, decrease the bottom → irresistible offer

**Most applicable to Halo:** Reframing the offer. Halo doesn't sell "Facebook ads" — it sells "20 new patients per month, automated, with no work on your end." Frame EVERYTHING through that lens.

## The Universal Patterns (Every Fast-Scaling Agency Did These)

### 1. They niched before they felt ready
Every agency owner who scaled says: "I wish I'd niched sooner." The instinct is to stay broad ("we work with any local business") to maximize opportunities. The reality: broad positioning means zero differentiation, longer sales cycles, and no word-of-mouth.

**Halo is already niched (healthcare). Double down. Don't drift.**

### 2. They used outbound to fund inbound
In the early days: cold outreach fills the pipeline while you build the brand. As you accumulate clients and results, content, referrals, and reputation start generating inbound. The fastest agencies do BOTH simultaneously — cold outreach now, content for inbound starting now, pays off in 6–12 months.

**For Halo:** Start documenting results and building a presence (LinkedIn, maybe YouTube) in parallel with cold outreach. Even a monthly newsletter to past contacts keeps Halo top of mind.

### 3. They obsessed over one metric: active retained clients
Not leads. Not revenue. Not MRR. **Active clients who are happy.** Because a happy client:
- Doesn't churn
- Refers others
- Pays more over time (upsells)
- Becomes a case study
- Makes your sales calls easier ("We worked with a practice just like yours...")

Every decision should be filtered through: "Does this help us keep clients happy and getting results?"

### 4. They fired bad clients
This sounds counterintuitive. But clients who are constantly unhappy, demand more than they pay for, or are bad fits drain time and morale from the clients worth keeping. Fast-scaling agencies ruthlessly protected their capacity for good clients.

**Signs a Halo client should be fired:**
- Demands constant changes to ads without basis
- Won't follow the system (won't set up Bland.ai, won't use GHL)
- Calls/emails outside normal hours consistently
- Blames the agency for problems that are theirs (bad phone handling, can't treat the volume)
- At or below minimum retainer and taking maximum time

### 5. They charged more than felt comfortable
The fastest growth came AFTER a price increase, not before. Because:
- Higher price = higher quality clients (fewer tire-kickers)
- Better margin = more bandwidth to deliver great service
- Clients who pay more invest more in making the relationship work

Halo at $1,950/month is the right starting price. The move to $2,500–$2,950 should happen as soon as the case studies support it.

### 6. They hired for specific outcomes, not general help
The worst agency hires: "I need help" → hire someone → vague role → person flounders.
The best agency hires: "I need 40 discovery calls booked this month" → hire SDR → clear metric → hire works or doesn't.

Every hire Halo makes should have a specific, measurable outcome tied to it within 30–60 days.

### 7. They used their clients' results as their primary marketing asset
Case studies close deals. Not your website. Not your logo. Not your pitch deck.

One genuine case study: "Dr. Renee was getting 2–3 new patients/month from referrals. 90 days after launching with Halo, she's consistently seeing 18–22 new patients/month" — that closes more deals than any ad.

**Halo's immediate priority:** Document Renee's results from day 1. Track everything. Build the case study. Make it shareable.
