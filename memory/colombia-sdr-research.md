## Why Colombia Works for This Role

- **English proficiency**: Colombia ranks #1 in LatAm for English skills (EF EPI). Bogotá, Medellín especially strong — natural accent, easy to understand on calls.
- **Time zone**: UTC-5 (no DST) — overlaps perfectly with US Eastern (same time) and Central (+1h). Works US business hours without the burnout that comes from Philippine night shifts.
- **Cost**: $800–$1,500/month USD for a strong bilingual SDR with base + commission. Roughly 1/3 the cost of a US hire.
- **Culture fit**: Colombian sales culture is hustle-oriented, relationship-driven, and persistent — maps well to cold calling healthcare.
- **Availability**: Strong supply of young, ambitious, English-speaking professionals who want remote USD income.

## Where to Hire

### Best Platforms
1. **LinkedIn** — Post job as remote; filter by Colombia + "English fluency" + sales. Premium job post = good reach (~$150-200/month, cancel after hire)
2. **Workana** (workana.com) — Colombia's top freelance/remote job board. Better than Upwork for LatAm. Free to post.
3. **GetOnBoard** (getonbrd.com) — Startup/tech-oriented LatAm job board. $0-99/post. Great for younger talent.
4. **Torre.ai** — AI-assisted LatAm hiring, talent profiles include skills assessments. Worth a look.
5. **Remote Colombia Facebook groups** — "Trabajo Remoto Colombia", "Inglés para trabajar" groups have active job seekers. Free.
6. **Referral network** — If you know any Colombian contractors or VAs, ask them. LatAm talent circles are tight.

### Avoid
- Upwork for this role — markup is too high and vetting is your problem anyway
- Generic US job boards — you'll drown in wrong applicants

## Screening Process (Recommended)

1. **Written application** — 3–5 Qs: Why sales? Describe a time you handled rejection. What do you know about healthcare marketing? (Filters for effort + English writing)
2. **Short video intro** (1–2 min) — Forces them to show up on camera, tests comfort. Bonus: you see their setup.
3. **Cold call role-play** (15 min Zoom) — You or Preston play a skeptical chiro receptionist. See how they handle objections, whether they're likeable.
4. **Reference check** — Previous employer or client. Even a brief one tells you a lot.
5. **Paid trial week** — 20–40 hours, paid. Real calls on a warm-ish list. Converts best candidates and weeds out the talkers.

## What to Look For (SDR Profile)

**Must haves:**
- Clear English with minimal accent (phone-safe)
- Persistent without being pushy — healthcare is relationship-driven
- Coachable — will follow a script, take feedback without ego
- Reliable internet + home office setup
- US business hours availability (9am–6pm ET)

**Nice to have:**
- Prior BDR/SDR experience in healthcare, SaaS, or insurance
- Experience with CRMs (GHL a bonus, but any CRM is fine)
- Familiar with cold email or LinkedIn outreach
- US calling experience specifically

**Red flags:**
- Vague answers about past rejection or difficult calls
- No interest in healthcare/medical field (will sound hollow on calls)
- Requires full-time salary guarantee without performance accountability
- Poor writing quality in application (signals English gap under pressure)
