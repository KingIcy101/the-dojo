# Re-Engagement Scripts — Suzanne (Dental) + Mike/Andy (Telehealth)

_Prepared by Halo, 2026-02-21._
_These are people who showed interest in Halo Marketing but didn't close. Priority: find out why and either re-open or learn from it._

---

## Context

- **Suzanne** — Dental. Was in pipeline, fell off. Unknown why she didn't close.
- **Mike/Andy** — Telehealth brain injury. Also fell off. Specialized niche ($2,950/mo pricing tier).

**Goal of these outreaches:**
1. Re-engage: maybe timing is better now, maybe objection can be addressed
2. Learn: if they're not interested, understand *why* — this is gold for the sales process

---

## Suzanne — Dental Re-Engagement

### Cold Email Option (warmer, lower friction)

**Subject:** Quick question, Suzanne

Hi Suzanne,

We spoke a little while back about getting more patients through the door at [practice name]. I wanted to reach out — not to pitch, just to check in.

A few things have shifted for us since we last talked: we've brought on a couple of dental practices and are seeing some solid early results. I wanted to make sure I didn't leave you hanging without a good update.

If the timing just wasn't right, I completely understand. Either way — was there something specific that felt off about moving forward? I'd genuinely find the feedback helpful as we refine how we work with dental practices.

Happy to jump on a 10-minute call if you're open to it, or just reply here if that's easier.

— Matt  
Halo Marketing

---

### Cold Call Option (if no email response)

**Opener:**
> "Hey Suzanne, it's Matt from Halo Marketing — we connected a few weeks back. Do you have 2 minutes? ... Great. I won't take up your time. I was just following up because I realized we left our last conversation a bit open-ended, and I wanted to close the loop properly — whether that means picking it back up or just understanding if something didn't fit."

**If she says "I just went with someone else":**
> "That makes total sense, and I'm glad you found something. Mind if I ask — what pushed you toward that decision? Was it pricing, the approach, timing, something else? I ask because I want to make sure we're giving future dental practices a clearer picture upfront."

**If she says "Not ready / budget / timing":**
> "Totally fair. If I stayed in touch quarterly — no pressure, just check-ins — would that be okay? And if there's a better time of year for dental practices to take this on, I'd love to know."

**If she seems open:**
> "Honestly, a lot has changed since we last talked. We've got a couple of early client results I can walk you through — would a 20-minute call be worth it to you? No obligation to move forward, I just want to make sure you have current info."

## Mike/Andy — Telehealth Brain Injury Re-Engagement

### Context Notes
- This is a specialized niche — brain injury telehealth is a higher trust sale
- Pricing tier: $2,950/month (reflect the complexity and specialization)
- They may have hesitated due to cost, uncertainty about patient acquisition in their specific niche, or timeline
- Two decision-makers (Mike + Andy) = more complex sale; one may have been ready, other not

### Cold Email Option

**Subject:** Following up — Halo Marketing

Hi Mike (and Andy),

I wanted to reach back out after our last conversation. I know we left things a bit open-ended.

Working with telehealth practices — especially in something as specialized as brain injury — is something we take seriously. It's not a plug-and-play situation like general chiropractic, and I want to make sure we only work together if we're confident we can actually move the needle for your practice.

A couple of things I wanted to address directly:

1. **Lead quality in specialized telehealth**: Patient acquisition for neurological conditions is different. We'd build your campaigns specifically around brain injury education and patient journeys — not generic healthcare ads.

2. **Proving it first**: We're open to discussing a limited pilot structure if that removes some of the uncertainty.

Would a short call be worth it to revisit? Or if you've already moved in a different direction, I'd appreciate any feedback you're willing to share.

— Matt  
Halo Marketing

---

### Cold Call Option

**Opener:**
> "Hey Mike / Andy, it's Matt from Halo Marketing. We had a conversation a little while back about patient acquisition for [practice name]. Do you have 2–3 minutes? ... I wanted to loop back because I realized we may not have addressed a few important questions for your specific situation."

**Key discovery questions to ask:**
1. "When we last talked, what was the main thing that made you hesitant to move forward?" → Listen. Don't defend.
2. "Is patient volume still something you're actively trying to grow right now, or has that priority shifted?"
3. "How are you currently getting new patients?"

**If price was the objection:**
> "I hear you on that. The $2,950 is designed for specialized telehealth because the campaigns are significantly more complex — but I'm curious if there's a structure that would make the risk feel lower on your side. Would a 60-day pilot with a defined outcome target be worth exploring?"

**If they've already moved on:**
> "Totally understand. What ended up being the deciding factor for you? I ask because telehealth brain injury is a niche we're specifically building expertise in, and I want to make sure we're presenting it the right way to practices like yours."

## General Notes on These Outreaches

**Mindset going in:**
- Don't lead with a pitch — lead with curiosity
- The goal of the first touchpoint is to re-open the door or collect the lesson
- Silence after the first touch is normal — follow up 3–5x over 2–3 weeks before moving them to "dead"

**Timing:**
- Best cold call windows: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–9am or 4–5pm local time (before/after patient hours)
- Dental: avoid Monday mornings (busiest) and Friday afternoons

**What to track (in GHL):**
- Date of outreach
- Method (call/email)
- Outcome (no answer, left VM, spoke to Suzanne/Mike/Andy, got info)
- Reason they didn't close (if discovered)
- Next follow-up date
