# Prism — Content Playbook

## Halo Brand Voice

**Tone:** Confident, direct, human. We're experts who don't need to shout about it.  
**Vocabulary:** Plain English. No buzzwords. "New patients" not "patient acquisition volume." "We run ads" not "we deploy integrated omnichannel strategies."  
**What we avoid:** Hype, vague promises, corporate speak, and anything that sounds like every other marketing agency.  
**What we lean into:** Specificity, proof, real numbers, honest explanations of how things work.

## Content Pillars (Halo's Own Channels)

1. **Education** — How digital marketing works for healthcare practices (builds trust + positions expertise)
2. **Results** — Client wins, lead costs, booking rates (proof)
3. **Behind the scenes** — How we build campaigns, what our process looks like (transparency)
4. **Industry take** — Opinion on trends, common mistakes, what most agencies get wrong

## Platform Strategy

### LinkedIn
- Audience: Practitioners, practice managers, healthcare entrepreneurs
- Format: Short professional posts, occasional long-form
- Frequency: 3x/week
- Best performing: "What most agencies don't tell you about healthcare ads" angle

### Instagram / Facebook
- Audience: Local practitioners + referral sources
- Format: Visual results posts, before/after (patient count, not appearance), reels
- Frequency: 4–5x/week
- Best performing: "Client results" + testimonial style

### Email (Prospect List)
- Audience: Cold-to-warm prospects from outreach sequence
- Format: Plain text, conversational
- Frequency: 2x/month (educational + one pitch)

## Client Content Guidelines

When writing content for a client's practice:
- Get their brand voice guide first (use the Brand Voice Guide skill)
- Write as if you ARE the practice — first person plural ("At [Practice], we believe...")
- Healthcare content rules: no outcome guarantees, "may help" language for conditions, always recommend consulting the provider
- Local SEO focus: include city + niche in headers and meta descriptions
