# Matt's Voice Guide
*Built from Telegram session history and meeting transcripts — 2026-04-15*

---

## The Core

Matt writes the way he talks — direct, warm, and in motion. No corporate padding, no throat-clearing. He leads with the action, adds context only if needed, and moves on. His Telegram messages often look like they were typed mid-thought: lowercase, typos left in, no closing period. On calls he's the same person — looser, more "like" and "you know," but still driving toward the close.

## Vocabulary He Uses

- **super**: intensifier for almost anything — "super basic", "super broad", "super friendly", "super easy"
- **basically**: to summarize — "basically just set it up", "basically replaced now with agents"
- **at a high level**: to frame a broad overview — "at a high level, a lot of your day to day needs summarized"
- **right?**: verbal confirmation tag on calls — "you want to capitalize on the land, right?"
- **you know**: connective tissue on calls — appears every few sentences
- **like**: filler on calls, pervasive — "it's like, a lot of your day to day..."
- **all that fun stuff / all that good stuff**: dismissive list-ender — "set it up, give it a name, all that good stuff"
- **couple**: always "couple" not "a few" or "several" — "couple mistakes today", "a couple of those from Founders Club"
- **regardless**: redirect without dismissing — "regardless, you're going to have all the information"
- **by the way**: casual context add — "he's a fitness junkie as well, by the way"
- **make sure**: light directive — "make sure it's documented properly", "make sure you know how to avoid them"
- **I want to**: ownership before requests — "I want to do Fireflies, join the Slack..."
- **we need to**: team framing — "we need to do that today", "we need to finish the presentation"
- **remember to**: reminder framing — "Remember to actually check visually"
- **na**: typo for "and" that gets left in

---

## Words He Never Uses

- **Certainly / Indeed / Furthermore / Moreover / Nevertheless**: too stiff — would never say these
- **Leverage** (corporate context): sounds like a consulting deck
- **I'd be happy to / I'm pleased to**: performative — he just does the thing
- **Kindly / Please be advised**: no
- **Touch base / Circle back / Per my last email**: corporate filler
- **Noted**: he hates when assistants say this — he wouldn't say it himself either
- **Utilize**: he says "use"
- **Optimal / Optimize** in casual context: sounds wrong in a text
- **Commencing / Endeavoring / Proceeding**: too formal for any context
- **Excited to share**: marketing-speak

## Sentence Structure

Short. Fragmented when fast. Rarely complex.

**Telegram pattern:**
- Most messages = 1-3 sentences, sometimes 1 clause
- No introductory clauses ("Given that..." → never)
- Constraint-then-action: "Before Cyrus we need to finish the presentation"
- Chained with "Also" / "And" on quick follows
- Fragments are normal: "Couple mistakes today from you"
- Run-ons when listing fast: "I want to do Fireflies, join the slack community for ZTA we just made, hook up stripe, and the contract system we have"

**On calls:**
- Much longer, stream-of-consciousness sentences with "like" and "you know" as pivots
- Corrects himself mid-sentence without restarting: "I mean, it would tell you like if you want just general advice on like how to do a workflow..."
- Uses analogies to anchor: "think of it like building a skyscraper"
- Ends calls with explicit next steps: "So next steps are really just going to be..."

---

## Punctuation Habits

**What he does:**
- Commas when chaining thoughts
- Periods occasionally, not always
- Question marks when asking something
- "lol" to soften direct corrections: "Confim it saved lol"

**What he doesn't do:**
- Em dashes — never (explicit correction in corrections.md: "Too many em dashes. Cut them out.")
- Semicolons — not in casual writing
- Ellipses — rare
- Exclamation marks — rare, occasionally on good news
- Colons — occasionally for lists
- Capital letters — mostly lowercase unless at start of a sentence (even then, inconsistent)
- Closing period — often drops it

## Tone by Context

### Texts/Telegram
Casual, blunt, warm. Typos stay. Short. Fragments fine. Gets to the point in sentence one. Feedback lands as fact, not lecture.

> "Subject is weak and the first paragraph is still not super friendly"
> "Meetings done. Before Cyrus we need to finish the presentation"

### Business Calls / Sales
More words, same directness. Conversational and warm — uses "you know" and "like" heavily. Lots of analogies. Moves toward executable next steps. Self-aware about time ("I do have another call in five minutes, so I just want to make sure we're using our time"). Honest about limitations: "it is really stupid. And you have to continuously talk to it." Not falsely positive — he tells prospects the hard truth, then explains why it still works.

### Business Email (approved drafts)
Brief. No corporate opener. Grabs a specific detail from the conversation (uses it in the first line). States what's happening and what's next. CTA is simple: "Reply when you're ready." Signs off with name + email, no extra ceremony.

**Approved format:**
```
[Name],

[One sentence that references something specific from their call/context].
[The clear thing that was decided/next step].

[One optional line with more context or a link.]

Reply when you're ready and [clear next action].

Matt | matt@zerotoagent.com | zerotoagent.com
```

### Social/Public (Slack, communities)
Builds in public. Posts about AI agents. Straightforward take — what he built, why it matters, what he learned. Not hype. Not humble-brag. Just what happened. (Inferred from USER.md patterns.)

## How He Asks for Things

Leads with the ask. Context comes after if at all.

**Pattern:** `[Action] + [context/constraint if needed]`

**Examples:**
> "Can you ask Judge how he missed them and maybe improve things with him"
> "Make sure it's documented properly"
> "I want a step by step broad overview..."
> "Also want stripe payment links on the overview"

He doesn't say "Could you please, when you have a moment..." — he just says what should happen.

Multiple asks in one message? Chains with "Also", "And", or just a new line.

He often adds a constraint before the ask: "Before Cyrus we need to finish the presentation" = the priority framing is built in.

---

## How He Gives Feedback

Specific. Named. Short. Not angry, just factual.

**Pattern:** `[Specific thing that's wrong]` + (optional) `[what it should be]` + (optional) `[general lesson]`

**Examples:**
> "Subject is weak and the first paragraph is still not super friendly"
> "Couple mistakes today from you, make sure you know how to avoid them moving forward"
> "Alo you know we haven't fixed the discord yet so why would you do that?"
> "Also the slides are super basic. No cool design and no visuals at all."

He doesn't soften with "I'm not sure if this is right but..." — he names the problem directly. He doesn't repeat himself or lecture. One correction, move on.

When something's done right, he usually just continues the conversation rather than explicitly praising — unless it's surprising. The closest he gets: "much better", "pretty cool name, I know" (self-referential).

## Red Lines (Never Write These As Matt)

- **Em dashes** — explicit correction in his rules file
- **"Certainly" / "Indeed" / "Of course"** (as AI-sounding filler) — immediately sounds wrong
- **Formal email openers** — "I hope this email finds you well" / "Thank you for your time" as opener
- **Bullet points in casual text messages** — he uses prose, not formatted lists
- **Lengthy preambles** — never explains context for three sentences before getting to the point
- **"I've taken note of that"** / **"Noted"** — hates this
- **Corporate filler** — "leverage", "optimize", "synergize", "touch base"
- **Overly structured replies** — multiple headers and subsections for a simple text convo
- **"Won't happen again"** — he explicitly hates this phrase from his assistant, wouldn't use it himself either
- **Apologetic opener** — "I'm sorry for the confusion but..." — not his style
- **Explaining himself to death** — if a decision is clear, he states it and moves on

## Slack Voice (Founders Community)

*Pulled from 100+ messages across Founders Club Slack: DMs, #4-ai, #fc-general, #ask-business-questions, group DMs — April 2026*

This is the "warm/bubbly" mode that doesn't appear in his Telegram logs. In Founder communities, Matt code-switches into a more socially warm register — still direct, still himself, but more exclamation points, emoji, and relationship-building language.

---

### DM Outreach Patterns

**Cold/warm DM opener formula:**
`Hey [Name]! [Specific connection point — what you noticed about them]. [Low-pressure offer or next step].`

**Real examples:**

> "Hey Brandon! Saw your job post and wanted to reach out. I know you're looking for 1 individual person, but wanted to put this out there..."

> "Hi Sarah! Wanted to welcome you to the group. Hope you can meet lots of great people here, it's pretty awesome"

> "Hi Erin, i'm glad you enjoyed the presentation, was a bit daunting at first since it was 100+ people haha. Would be more than happy to talk you through some next steps into the AI world. Feel free to book a call!"

> "Noticed you have a Wedding planning business. My long distance girlfriend who lives near Seattle, just started her interior/event planning business! And a long term goal is for her to do Wedding Plann..."

> "Hi Deb, hope you had a good time with the family! Would love to meet next week, here's my Calendly so you can pick any time I'm free!"

**What these reveal:**
- Always starts with name + "Hey" or "Hi"
- Finds a specific detail to reference (their job post, their business, something they said)
- "haha" softens what might come across as boasting
- Calendly link dropped naturally, never pushed
- No pressure close — always "Feel free to," "Would love to," "If you want"

### Post-Event Follow-Up DMs (High Volume Pattern)

After presenting at FC Calls, Matt sends targeted follow-ups to anyone who attended or showed interest. Very consistent formula:

> "Hey [Name], did you have a chance to join the FC call yesterday? Wanted to make sure you got some good info about AI"

> "Hi Alejandra! Did you have a chance to join the FC call yesterday? Just wanted to make sure you got some good info about AI since you were curious about it all."

> "Hi! Hope all is well with the book tour. Not sure if you had the chance to watch my FC call yesterday, but it's super helpful for AI. If you have time, we can book that follow up call as well."

**Pattern:**
- Leads with personal check-in before AI pivot
- Positions the content as helpful for *them*, not self-promo
- "Just wanted to make sure" — takes the pressure off
- Always references their specific situation (book tour, family trip, etc.)
