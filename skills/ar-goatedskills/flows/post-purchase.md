# Post-Purchase

## Overview

**Trigger:** Order confirmed / payment captured
**Goal:** Eliminate buyer's remorse, set up successful first use, build loyalty, seed the repeat order
**Length:** 4-5 emails over 21-30 days
**Exit:** Repeat purchase → new post-purchase flow for the new order
**Target:** 25-40% repeat purchase rate from first-time buyers

---

## Email 1 — Immediately (within 1 hour of order confirmed)
**Subject angle:** Order confirmed + anticipation building
**Preview text:** Order number + what happens next

**Copy angle:**
- Confirm the order (order number, product name, estimated delivery)
- This is not a dry transactional email — use it to build excitement
- Reinforce the decision: "Great choice — here's why you'll love it"
- Short piece of content that sets up success (what to expect, how to use the product)
- Set delivery expectations to prevent anxiety and support tickets
- Secondary ask: follow on social / join community

**Tone:** Warm and excited. Not robotic and transactional.

**Note:** Many ESPs separate transactional vs. marketing emails. In Klaviyo, customize the Order Confirmation template to add brand voice and the copy elements above.

---

## Email 2 — Day 3-5 (before or just after delivery)
**Subject angle:** Product education / "how to get the most out of [product]"
**Preview text:** Quick tip or usage guide teaser

**Copy angle:**
- Teach them how to use the product correctly — this alone reduces returns significantly
- Lead with the outcome they want, then the method
- Short how-to (3-5 steps, video link if available)
- Set expectations: what they'll notice in the first week
- Tip that makes the product feel more premium or effective
- Invite them to reply with questions: "We're real people — hit reply"

**CTA:** Read the full guide / Watch the tutorial / See tips

---

## Email 3 — Day 7-10 (after delivery)
**Subject angle:** Review request / "How's it going?"
**Preview text:** "We'd love to know what you think"

**Copy angle:**
- Genuine check-in — warm, not automated-feeling
- "We'd love to know how you're getting on with [product]"
- Make the review ask easy: link directly to review page
- Explain why reviews matter (they help future customers make the right decision)
- If they have a problem → make it easy to contact support before they leave a bad review
- Optional: loyalty incentive for leaving a review

**CTA:** Leave a review / Share your experience

---

## Email 4 — Day 14-21
**Subject angle:** Cross-sell / "customers who bought X also love Y"
**Preview text:** Complementary product teaser

**Copy angle:**
- "You've had [product] for a few weeks — here's what pairs with it"
- Lead with the customer benefit of the complementary product
- 2-3 complementary SKUs with brief benefit descriptions
- Social proof: "Customers who bought [original] also love [cross-sell]"
- Loyalty angle if applicable: bundle discount, subscription offer, loyalty points

**CTA:** Shop the pairing / Complete your routine / Add to your order

---

## Email 5 — Day 22-28 (consumable products only)
**Subject angle:** Running low? Replenishment reminder
**Preview text:** "Based on typical usage, you might be getting close"

**Copy angle:**
- Relevant only for consumables — skip for non-consumables
- "Based on normal use, you've got about [X] days of [product] left"
- Easy one-click reorder link
- Subscribe-and-save pitch if available: never run out, save [X]%

**CTA:** Reorder now / Subscribe and save [X]%

---

## Suppression Logic

- Suppress from welcome flow if they purchase during welcome sequence
- Suppress from cart abandonment when order is confirmed
- If customer orders again during post-purchase flow, restart post-purchase for new order

---

## A/B Test Priorities

1. Email 2: Tutorial / how-to vs. community / social proof email
2. Email 3: Review ask at Day 7 vs. Day 10
3. Email 4: Cross-sell grid vs. single hero complementary product
4. Email 1: Transactional tone vs. warm brand voice (measure long-term repeat purchase impact)

---

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Good |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Repeat purchase rate | 25% | 35-40%+ |
| Review conversion (Email 3) | 5-10% | 15%+ |
| Cross-sell CTR (Email 4) | 8-12% | 15%+ |
| Return rate | Track vs. pre-flow baseline | Decreasing |
| Time-to-second-order | Track vs. baseline | Shortening |
