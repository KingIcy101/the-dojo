# Winback

## Overview

**Trigger:** Last purchase date > 60/90/120 days ago (set threshold based on your average purchase cycle)
**Goal:** Reactivate dormant customers before they forget you exist. Compete with inertia, not competitors.
**Length:** 3 emails over 14-21 days
**Exit:** Any purchase exits the flow. No engagement after Email 3 → move to Sunset.
**CVR target:** 3-8%

---

## Setting Your Winback Threshold

| Average purchase cycle | Winback trigger |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| < 30 days | 60 days no purchase |
| 30-60 days | 90 days no purchase |
| 60-120 days | 120-150 days no purchase |
| 120+ days | 180+ days no purchase |

Do not trigger winback before a customer has had a reasonable opportunity to repurchase naturally.

---

## Email 1 — Day 0 (trigger fires)
**Subject angle:** "We miss you" — warm, no guilt, no pressure
**Preview text:** Something human and genuine, not promotional

**Copy angle:**
- Open warmly — acknowledge their absence without accusation
- Reference their history briefly: "It's been a while since [product] was part of your routine"
- No hard pitch — this is the re-introduction
- Remind them of what they had: what they bought, what it did for them
- Soft invitation back: "When you're ready, we're here"
- Make re-engagement effortless — one button, one action

**CTA:** Come back and see what's new / Browse [brand] again

**Tone:** Warm, slightly vulnerable, human. Not promotional. Not guilt-tripping.

---

## Email 2 — Day 7
**Subject angle:** Genuine reason to return — new product, update, or best offer
**Preview text:** Specific news hook or offer teaser

**Copy angle:**
- Give them a real reason to come back: new product, updated formula, bestseller they haven't tried
- If nothing new: a customer transformation story (someone who came back and is glad they did)
- Introduce a winback offer: discount, free shipping, or free gift
- Soft urgency: "This offer is for customers we haven't seen in a while — it won't be here forever"

**CTA:** Claim your welcome-back offer / See what's new

---

## Email 3 — Day 14-21
**Subject angle:** Last chance — final attempt
**Preview text:** "This is the last time we'll reach out for a while"

**Copy angle:**
- Brief and honest: this is the last email in this series
- No hard sell — state clearly that you won't keep emailing if they're not interested
- Best offer of the series (strongest discount or most generous incentive)
- Alternative to unsubscribing: "Update your preferences here [link]" — moves them to low-frequency instead of losing them completely

**CTA:** Claim your [X]% off before it expires / Update your preferences

---

## Suppression Logic

- Suppress anyone currently in replenishment flow (they're still active)
- Suppress VIPs — run a separate VIP winback with a higher-value offer
- Suppress if they've gone through a winback sequence in the last 6 months
- Suppress if they previously unsubscribed

---

## Segmentation by Purchase History

| Segment | Winback approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| 1x buyer | Brand re-introduction, lower offer threshold |
| 2-3x buyer | Reference purchase history, stronger offer |
| VIP (4+ orders) | Personal tone, most generous offer, dedicated sequence |

---

## Copy Rules

**Never use:**
- "Where did you go?" (accusatory)
- "You've been ignoring us" (passive-aggressive)
- "We're disappointed" (guilt)

**Always use:**
- "We miss having you around"
- "Whenever you're ready"
- "No pressure — we're here if you need us"

---

## A/B Test Priorities

1. Email 1: No offer vs. soft offer (test revenue vs. unsubscribe trade-off)
2. Email 2: New product hook vs. social proof vs. discount hook
3. Email 3: Hard expiration urgency vs. soft preferences-update angle
4. Subject lines: Name personalization vs. curiosity gap vs. product reference
