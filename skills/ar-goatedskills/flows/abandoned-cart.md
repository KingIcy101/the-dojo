# Abandoned Cart

## Overview

**Trigger:** Item added to cart (or checkout started) → no purchase within 1 hour
**Goal:** Complete the purchase. Highest-intent flow — match that urgency. This is recovery, not nurture.
**Length:** 3 emails over 48-72 hours
**Exit:** Purchase at any point exits immediately
**CVR target:** 10-15%

---

## Email 1 — 1 hour after abandonment
**Subject angle:** Simple, direct cart reminder — no tricks
**Preview text:** Product name + cart reminder

**Copy angle:**
- Immediately show their cart (dynamic product block with image, name, price)
- One-line opener: "You left something behind" or "Your cart is waiting"
- Address the most common abandonment reason for your category upfront:
  - State your shipping policy clearly
  - Include your guarantee / free returns
  - Include security/trust signals
- No urgency yet — keep it simple and frictionless
- Make completing checkout the path of least resistance

**CTA:** Complete your order / Return to cart / Finish checkout

---

## Email 2 — 24 hours later
**Subject angle:** Social proof + light urgency
**Preview text:** Review snippet or "X people bought this today"

**Copy angle:**
- Reinforce the decision they were about to make
- Lead with outcome: what life looks like after buying this product
- 2-3 outcome-focused customer reviews for the cart item
- Introduce genuine scarcity if applicable: "Only X left in stock"
- Address the #1 objection for your product category
- Offer free shipping if not already offered and margin allows

**CTA:** Get it before it sells out / Complete your purchase

---

## Email 3 — 48-72 hours later
**Subject angle:** Final nudge — offer or last call
**Preview text:** Discount code or expiration signal

**Copy angle:**
- This is your last email in the sequence — say so briefly
- Offer your best incentive: % discount, free gift, free shipping
- Create real urgency: the offer expires in 24 hours
- Brief — they've seen two emails already; respect their time
- Final risk reversal (money-back guarantee, easy returns)
- If avoiding discount: use a story-driven final push or highest-impact review instead

**CTA:** Claim your [X]% off / Use code [CODE] at checkout

---

## Suppression Logic

- Exit flow immediately on purchase at any email
- Suppress if customer purchased a different item during the sequence
- Suppress if customer received a cart abandonment email in the last 14 days
- Suppress VIP segment from Email 3 discount (reward with service, not discounts)

---

## Shipping Objection Note

Cart abandonment is often a shipping cost problem, not a messaging problem. If CVR is low despite strong copy:
- Test free shipping threshold ("Free shipping on orders over $X")
- Mention shipping cost explicitly in Email 1 and either justify or eliminate it
- Survey recent abandoners to confirm root cause before rewriting copy

---

## A/B Test Priorities

1. Email 1 delay: 30 min vs. 1 hour vs. 2 hours
2. Email 3: Discount vs. no discount (measure revenue impact net of discount cost)
3. Subject lines: Direct ("Your cart expires soon") vs. curiosity ("Did something go wrong?")
4. Email 1: Minimal/text-only layout vs. full product image layout

---

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Good |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Flow CVR | 10% | 15%+ |
| Email 1 open rate | 40-50% | 55-60% |
| Email 1 CVR | 5-8% | 10%+ |
| Email 3 CVR | 2-4% | 5%+ |
