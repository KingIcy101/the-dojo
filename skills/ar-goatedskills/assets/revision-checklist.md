# Revision Checklist

Run this before you send the draft.

## 1. Opening

- Does the piece open with a real observation, memory, quote, or scene?
- If not, replace the generic opener with something tangible.

## 2. Specificity

- Are there at least one or two concrete details when the format allows for them?
- Replace abstract nouns with places, people, objects, or moments.

## 3. Rhythm

- Break up any run of equally polished sentences.
- Add a short sentence after a longer one if the cadence is too flat.
- Use commas, colons, or periods instead of em dashes.

## 4. Emotional Honesty

- Does the draft admit uncertainty, tension, or mixed feeling where appropriate?
- If the piece feels overly certain, let some doubt or contradiction back in.

## 5. Anti-AI Sweep

- Remove banned words and banned structures.
- Remove fake authority.
- Remove generic transitions that sound imported from business writing.

## 6. Ending

- End on a real note, not a slogan.
- A modest insight, open question, or partial resolution is usually better than a big takeaway.

## 7. Read-Aloud Test

- Read it aloud.
- If you would never say a sentence in conversation, rewrite it.
- If the voice sounds performative, simplify it.
