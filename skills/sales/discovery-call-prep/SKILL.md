# Discovery Call Prep Skill

When triggered, do NOT ask questions. Start researching immediately. Deliver a finished brief.

---

## Research Sequence (run in order)

### 1. Google the prospect
```
[Name] chiropractor [city] [state]
[Practice name] reviews
[Name] DC linkedin
```
Look for: years in practice, solo vs group, website quality, Google rating, review count, Facebook presence.

### 2. Check their website
- Professional or DIY (Wix/Squarespace/GoDaddy)?
- Does it have a booking system?
- Does it have patient testimonials?
- Mobile-friendly?
- Speed estimate (does it load fast or feel slow)?
- What's their core offer — adjustments only, or wellness/nutrition/massage too?

### 3. Check their Google Business Profile
- Rating + review count (proxy for patient volume)
- How recent are reviews? (Active = healthy practice)
- Are they responding to reviews? (Signals how engaged they are)
- Photos updated recently?

### 4. Check Facebook / Instagram
- Do they run ads? (Check Facebook Ad Library: https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/?q=[name])
- Last post date — active or dead?
- Follower count
- Engagement rate on posts

### 5. Check Google Ads
Search for their practice name — do they appear in paid results? Are competitors running ads in their area?

## Talk Track

### Opening (first 2 min)
"Hey [Name], thanks for making time. Before I jump in — I did a little research on your practice. Quick question: are most of your new patients coming from referrals right now, or are you getting any from search/online?"

*[Whatever they say, pivot to:]*
"Yeah, that's actually really common for practices at your stage. What we see is..."

### Discovery Questions (pick 3-4)
1. "How many new patients are you looking to bring in per month?"
2. "Have you tried Facebook or Google ads before — what happened?"
3. "What does your current marketing look like — is anyone managing it for you?"
4. "If you doubled new patients in 90 days, what would that mean for the practice?"
5. "What's your biggest frustration with how you're getting patients right now?"

### The Pivot to Halo
"So what we do at Halo is [simple 2-sentence version]. We're specifically focused on [specialty] practices — we don't work with everyone. The results we get for [similar practice type] are [specific outcome: X new patients/month, etc.]."

### Handling Objections
**"I need to think about it"**
→ "Totally fair. What's the main thing you'd want to think through? Is it the investment, or is it more about whether this works for your type of practice?"

**"I already tried ads and it didn't work"**
→ "What happened? ... Yeah, that's usually a targeting/offer problem, not an ads problem. We've seen that a lot with practices that ran ads without the right follow-up system."

**"It's too expensive"**
→ "What's one new patient worth to you lifetime? ... So at [X], you need [Y] new patients to break even. We average [Z] for practices like yours. Does that math work?"

### Close
"Here's what I'd suggest — let's do a 30-day pilot. We'll set up the ads, the landing page, and the follow-up system. If you don't see [X results] in 30 days, we'll refund you. Sound fair?"

## Quick Reminders
- Payment before service. Always.
- 3-month minimum. Month-to-month after.
- Never promise a specific patient number — promise a system.
- If they're a good fit, set next steps ON THE CALL. Don't leave it open.
- If they're Halo client material AND they order supplements — flag for Kargo (dual relationship).

## Briefing Rules

- Research takes 5-10 min. Don't rush it — a good brief closes deals.
- If you can't find much online, that IS the insight: "minimal online presence = huge opportunity"
- Always include the talk track. Matt shouldn't have to think on the call.
- Flag any red flags clearly — a bad client is worse than no client.
- Lead with what THEY care about, not what Halo offers.
- The goal of the discovery call is to book the close call, not to close on the spot (unless momentum is strong).
